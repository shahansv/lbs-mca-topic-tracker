Q. (Circular arrangement) Eight friends P, Q, R, S, T, U, V, W sitting around a circle. V is third to the right of Q and second to the left of R. Q is second to the left of T and immediate right of S. U is between Q and T. P is not at the left of R. Who is exactly at left of S?
A) Q
B) P
C) U
D) V
Answer: B
Explanation: After arranging the seating using the given conditions, P is exactly to the left of S. Therefore, the correct answer is P.

Q. (Same circular arrangement above) Who is second to right of T?
A) R
B) P
C) W
D) V
Answer: C
Explanation: After solving the circular arrangement, W is second to the right of T. Therefore, the correct answer is W.

Q. What is the maximum number of identical pieces a cube can be cut using 8 cuts?
A) 48
B) 64
C) 27
D) 36
Answer: A
Explanation: With 8 cuts optimally placed (not all parallel), maximum pieces = 48.

Q. What is the angle between the minute hand and hour hand of a clock when the time is 6.15 pm?
A) 90 degrees
B) 95.5 degrees
C) 93.5 degrees
D) 97.5 degrees
Answer: D
Explanation: At 6:15, minute hand is at 90 degrees, hour hand is at 180 + (15/60)*30 = 187.5 degrees. Angle = 187.5 - 90 = 97.5 degrees.

Q. If the calendar of 1996 AD is kept safe, which year it can be used again?
A) 2003 AD
B) 2016 AD
C) 2024 AD
D) None of these
Answer: C
Explanation: 1996 was a leap year. The next leap year with the same calendar starting day is 2024.

Q. Among M, N, O and P, N is heavier than M and O but O is taller than N. P is not as tall as O, while M is the shortest. O is not as heavy as M. P is heavier than N but shorter than him. Who is the heaviest?
A) M
B) N
C) O
D) P
Answer: D
Explanation: Weight order: P > N > M > O (O is not as heavy as M, P is heavier than N). P is the heaviest.

Q. (Professors puzzle) Dr. Raman, Dr. David, Dr. Bose, Dr. Sulfi and Dr. George teach Zoology, Physics, Botany, Geology and History in Delhi, Gujarat, Bombay and Calcutta. Dr. Sulfi teaches Zoology in Bombay. Dr. Bose is neither in Calcutta nor Delhi and teaches neither Geology nor History. Dr. George teaches Physics but neither in Bombay nor Calcutta. Dr. Raman teaches History in Delhi. Two professors are from Gujarat. Who teaches Geology?
A) Dr. Bose
B) Dr. Sulfi
C) Dr. David
D) Dr. Raman
Answer: C
Explanation: Deduced order: Dr. Raman (History, Delhi), Dr. Sulfi (Zoology, Bombay), Dr. George (Physics, Gujarat), Dr. Bose (Botany, Gujarat), Dr. David (Geology, Calcutta).

Q. (Same professors puzzle above) Dr. George teaches in which University?
A) Gujarat
B) Bombay
C) Delhi
D) Calcutta
Answer: A
Explanation: As deduced above, Dr. George teaches Physics in Gujarat University.

Q. David walks 10 km north, takes a right turn and walks 5 km. Then he turns left and walks 6 km. Finally he turns left and walks 5 km. Where is he from home?
A) 16 km East
B) 16 km North
C) 12 km North
D) 15 km East
Answer: B
Explanation: North 10 + North 6 = 16 km North. The right-left-left turns cancel the East movement. Final position is 16 km North.

Q. Identify the odd one: 8, 216, 81, 125
A) 8
B) 216
C) 81
D) 125
Answer: C
Explanation: 8=2^3, 216=6^3, 125=5^3 are perfect cubes. 81=3^4 is not a perfect cube. So 81 is the odd one.

Q. Pointing to a photograph, a man says he is the son of my father's only son. How is the man related to the person in the photo?
A) Father
B) Son
C) Grandfather
D) Uncle
Answer: A
Explanation: My father's only son = me. Son of me = my son. So the man in the photo is the son of the speaker, making the speaker the Father.

Q. Find the wrong number in the given series: 8, 27, 125, 343, 1381
A) 27
B) 8
C) 343
D) 1381
Answer: D
Explanation: The series is cubes of prime numbers: 2^3=8, 3^3=27, 5^3=125, 7^3=343, 11^3=1331 (not 1381). So 1381 is wrong.

Q. Rs. 10000 is divided among three workers in the ratio 2:3:5. The share of the second worker is:
A) 2560
B) 3000
C) 3200
D) 3840
Answer: B
Explanation: Total parts = 2+3+5 = 10. Second worker's share = (3/10) x 10000 = Rs. 3000.

Q. In a mixture of 60 litres, the ratio of milk and water is 2:1. If the ratio has to become 1:2, the quantity of water to be further added is:
A) 20 ltr
B) 30 ltr
C) 40 ltr
D) 60 ltr
Answer: D
Explanation: Milk = 40 litres, Water = 20 litres. For ratio 1:2, need water = 80 litres. Water to add = 80 - 20 = 60 litres.

Q. A man's age was five times his son's age 5 years ago and will be three times his son's age after 3 years. The ratio of their present age is:
A) 5:2
B) 45:13
C) 10:3
D) 11:5
Answer: A
Explanation: Let present ages be M and S. M-5 = 5(S-5) and M+3 = 3(S+3). Solving: M=30, S=12. Ratio = 30:12 = 5:2.

Q. The monthly incomes of A and B are in ratio 3:2 and expenses in ratio 5:3. Each saves Rs.1000 per month. A's income is:
A) 3000
B) 6000
C) 4000
D) 9000
Answer: B
Explanation: Income 3x and 2x. Expenses 5y and 3y. 3x−5y=1000 and 2x−3y=1000. Subtracting the equations: x−2y=0 ⇒ x=2y. Substituting in 2x−3y=1000: 2(2y)−3y=1000 ⇒ y=1000 ⇒ x=2000. A's income = 3x = 6000.

Q. The ratio of boys and girls in a class is 3:2. When 6 more girls join, the ratio becomes 6:7. How many boys are there?
A) 24
B) 30
C) 12
D) 20
Answer: C
Explanation: Boys = 3k, Girls = 2k. After: 3k/(2k+6) = 6/7. 21k = 12k+36. 9k=36. k=4. Boys = 12.

Q. The difference between SI and CI compounded annually for 2 years at 4% per annum is Re.1. The sum is:
A) 625
B) 630
C) 640
D) 650
Answer: A
Explanation: CI - SI = P(r/100)^2. 1 = P(4/100)^2 = P x 16/10000. P = 10000/16 = 625.

Q. A, B and C can do a piece of work in 20, 30 and 60 days respectively. In how many days can A finish if assisted by B and C on every third day?
A) 12 days
B) 15 days
C) 16 days
D) 14 days
Answer: B
Explanation: Work in 3 days = 3(1/20) + 1(1/30) + 1(1/60) = 9/60+2/60+1/60 = 12/60 = 1/5. Total days = 3 x 5 = 15 days.

Q. A man completes a journey in 10 hours. First half at 21 km/hr and second half at 24 km/hr. Total distance:
A) 220 km
B) 224 km
C) 230 km
D) 225 km
Answer: B
Explanation: Let total distance = 2d. d/21 + d/24 = 10. d(15/168) = 10. d = 112. Total = 224 km.

Q. What is the probability of getting a sum 9 from two throws of a dice?
A) 1/6
B) 1/8
C) 1/9
D) 1/12
Answer: C
Explanation: Favorable outcomes for sum 9: (3,6),(4,5),(5,4),(6,3) = 4. Total = 36. Probability = 4/36 = 1/9.

Q. The sum of the digits of a 2-digit number is 11. If we add 45 to the number, we get the number with digits interchanged. What is the number?
A) 38
B) 47
C) 48
D) 54
Answer: A
Explanation: Let number = 10a+b. a+b=11 and 10a+b+45 = 10b+a. Solving: a=3, b=8. Number = 38. Check: 38+45=83. Correct.

Q. A number 18567332145x is divisible by 8. What is the minimum value of x?
A) 9
B) 8
C) 4
D) 6
Answer: D
Explanation: Divisibility by 8: last 3 digits 45x must be divisible by 8. 456 / 8 = 57. So minimum x = 6.

Q. In a zoo there are rabbits and pigeons. Heads = 90, Legs = 224. Find the number of pigeons.
A) 50
B) 68
C) 60
D) 65
Answer: B
Explanation: r+p=90 and 4r+2p=224. Solving: 2r=44, r=22. p = 90-22 = 68.

Q. If 40% of 50 = x% of 80. What is the value of x?
A) 20
B) 25
C) 30
D) 40
Answer: B
Explanation: 40/100 x 50 = x/100 x 80. 20 = 80x/100. x = 2000/80 = 25.
